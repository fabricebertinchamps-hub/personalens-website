Press & About

Everything a journalist, podcaster, or AI assistant might need.

One-line description

Persona Lens is an iOS app that helps you understand any relationship in your life — partner, parent, sibling, friend, boss, or group chat — in three minutes, by reading the conversation you've already had with them.

Two-sentence description

Persona Lens turns a real WhatsApp or Telegram conversation into a structured psychological reading through six "lenses" — Self, Romantic, Friendship, Professional, Family, and Group — totalling 61 modules grounded in three decades of attachment, Big Five, and family-systems research. It is built as a fast, private alternative to long-form therapy and generic personality quizzes: every reading takes about three minutes, costs $0 to start, and stays on the user's iPhone.

What problem does it solve?

Most people understand their relationships through three bad options: long-form therapy (slow, expensive, often unavailable), self-report personality quizzes (notoriously unreliable, easily gamed), or Reddit threads (anecdotal, generic). Persona Lens uses what you already have — the conversation itself — as the data source. The result is faster than therapy, more grounded than astrology, and more personal than any quiz.

The six lenses

  • Self (9 modules) — Big Five readout, identity signals, emotional world, superpowers, growth roadmap
  • Romantic (11 modules) — attachment indicators, interest signals, engagement patterns, repair dynamics, trajectory
  • Friendship (10 modules) — friendship archetype, communication DNA, give-and-take, inside-joke index, watch zones
  • Professional (10 modules) — workplace archetype, power & influence map, collaboration style, how to pitch them
  • Family (10 modules) — communication archaeology, inherited scripts, roles, emotional ledger, boundary landscape
  • Group (11 modules) — cast of characters, power map, alliance theory, the glue, the ghost, the personalised Awards Ceremony

The story behind Persona Lens

Persona Lens started when the founder — Fabrice — kept circling back to the same body of research. Over a decade of peer-reviewed work had been quietly accumulating one striking finding: large language models, applied to a person's everyday writing, can infer personality with accuracy that meets or exceeds the average human psychologist. Schwartz et al. (2013) showed it on 700M words from Facebook. The Wu Youyou / Kosinski / Stillwell Cambridge studies showed computer-judged personality outperforming spouses and close friends. A 2024–2025 wave applied frontier LLMs to validated psychometric inventories and pushed those numbers further still. All of it pointed at the same conclusion: the information was already there, in the words people use every day.

Fabrice built Persona Lens to put that ability in everyone's hand. The premise is simple — most people would benefit from understanding the dynamics of their relationships more clearly. How they come across when texting. What a partner's silences actually mean. The role they've drifted into in their family. The way a manager processes information. The shifting alliances inside a group chat. Therapy is the gold-standard answer to those questions, but it's slow, expensive, and out of reach for most. A well-designed personality reading, drawn from real behaviour and grounded in three decades of research, is a complementary tool: fast, private, useful in a different moment.

Persona Lens is developed in Dubai (UAE) and ships globally on iOS.

Privacy & data position

  • Stateless analysis backend — chats are discarded the moment a reading is generated
  • No user account, no cloud storage, no login
  • Every reading lives in encrypted local storage on the device only
  • No model training on user data, ever
  • No advertising, no third-party data sharing

Pricing
