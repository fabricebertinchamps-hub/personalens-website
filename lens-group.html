Group Lens analyses the whole group. The Modules

What's inside a Group reading.

Eleven modules — but the one everyone screenshots is at the bottom. Skip down if you want to.

  1. Cast of CharactersEvery member of the group gets a one-line characterisation and a named archetype.
  2. Power MapWho drives the conversation, who steers it, who fades. Visual, named, gentle.
  3. Alliance TheoryThe hidden sub-groups inside the bigger one. The two-person side chat that runs the main chat.
  4. The GlueThe one (or two) members who keep the chat alive. The receipt for why the rest of you still show up.
  5. The GhostThe lurker. Named without judgement — sometimes ghosts are the safety net.
  6. Group VibeBanter / philosophical / logistics-heavy / loving / chaotic. With the percentage split.
  7. Inside Jokes IndexHow many in-jokes are alive, and who keeps them alive.
  8. Patterns & LoopsThe conversations the group has had four times this year. With different names.
  9. TrajectoryIs this chat growing, steady, or quietly slowing? With one move that would change it.
  10. Roles & ScriptsThe mum, the planner, the ghost, the chaos agent, the late-night philosopher, the catalyst.
  11. The Awards CeremonyThe signature output. Every member gets a personalised award. Built for screenshot. (Examples below.)

What Group Lens returns · Part 1

Cast of Characters.

Group Lens names each member of the chat. Not by their handle — by their role.

  1. Pia · The Chaos AgentDrives the chat at full speed and is, somehow, almost always right. Decisions get made because she refuses to let them sit. Has typed "I am buying flights at midnight" at least three times this year.
  2. Marta · The LogisticianThe reason this group has not yet missed a flight. Translates feelings into spreadsheets. Will gently re-share the link if you didn't read it the first time.
  3. Sara · The HeartThe emotional centre. Says "I love you" in the chat more than the rest of the group combined. Cries with affection.
  4. Léa · The CloserComes in late, ends the debate in one line. Soft on people, hard on bad ideas. Has veto power and uses it sparingly.
  5. Yara · The RealistThe one who remembers that the rest of you have jobs. The grounding wire. Annoyed and beloved in equal measure.
  6. Camille · The Catch-Up QueenReads 200 messages at 11 PM and replies with one perfectly compressed summary. Always agrees with the right person.

Useful to know

What Group Lens does — and doesn't.

What Group Lens does

  • Names every participant in the chat with their own archetype
  • Maps power, alliances, and group vibe
  • Produces a personalised Award for each member
  • Builds shareable cards explicitly designed for the group's own chat
  • Works on chats of 3 to ~25 members

What Group Lens doesn't

  • It does not pick a "best member" — there are no rankings
  • It does not produce content meant to hurt anyone
  • It does not name the people by their phone numbers — only by the names already in the chat
  • It does not work well on chats under ~50 messages
  • It is the lens that takes the longest to generate — give it ~90 seconds
